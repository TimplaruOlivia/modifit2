    TESTING REPORT

     

     The goal of the testing was to see whether our protoype of our website is cohesive and whether users understood who it was meant for, what it was meant for and how easy it was to navigate.

    TESTING METHODS 

    We conducted four user tests, all with women within our target age group. Two of the participants were hockey players, providing valuable insights from that perspective.The other two participants were unfamiliar with field hockey,and we focused on testing how intuitive it was for them to navigate it. Two of the interviews were conducted on campus and two were conducted in a quiet setting outside of the campus. We offered them our laptop with the website open and we audio recorded them on our phone. These are the questions and tasks we used for the users

    These are the questions and tasks we used for the users:

  • First Impressions

    • 1.What is your first impression of this website?

    2.What do you think this website is about?

    3.Who do you think this website is for?

    4.What catches your eye first?

  • Tasks:

    • 1.Spend 1‚Äì2 minutes just exploring the homepage. Tell me what stands out to you.

    2.Without clicking anything, describe what you think this website offers.

    3.Find a product that interests you and walk me through how you would buy it.

    4.Add a product to your cart (or pretend to). What would you do next?

    5.Find the price of one of the new arrivals.

    6.Can you check what the company‚Äôs mission and values are?

    7.Can you try to get in contact with the company?

    8.Try sending a message through the contact form.

    9.Find the company‚Äôs Instagram page.

    10.Watch the video on the ‚ÄòWhy Choose Us‚Äô section. What‚Äôs your impression afterward?

    11.Find your way from the homepage to the shop, then to the about us page.

    12.See if you can return to the homepage from where you are now.

  • Post User Testing

    • 1.How easy was it to find what you were looking for?

    2.Did anything confuse you or feel out of place?

    3.Was it clear how to get back to the homepage?

    4.Would you trust this site enough to make a purchase? Why or why not?

    5.Is anything missing from the product pages that you would expect to see?

    6.How does the design make you feel?

    7.Do the colors, fonts, and images fit the brand?

    8.Is the site visually appealing to you?

    9.Does anything feel too cluttered or too empty?

    10.Was it clear what each section was for?

  • Open-Ended Questions

    • 1.What do you like most about the site?

    2.What do you like least?

    3.If you could change one thing, what would it be?

    Marketing

    Context of campaign and promotional activities

    This campaign promotes Modifit's standout product, a vacuum-sealed fully customizable mouthguard designed especially for female field hockey players. The campaign celebrates individuality, empowerment, and team pride while highlighting how Modifit gear enhances both performance and identity on the field. Through motivational content and community-building visuals on Instagram, we aim to grow brand awareness and foster loyalty within our audience. The campaign includes posting 3 times a week, highlighting product details and team moments, and sharing stories according to the content calendar to boost engagement. Through Instagram, we are able to:

    • Build a consistent brand identity visually
    • Foster direct community interaction via DM‚Äôs, comments, and polls.

    By April 11th, the campaign aimed to reach the following:

    • Objectives: 100 accounts reached per post
    • Response: 200-250 new followers, 30 likes per post, 5 comments
    • Behavior: Ensure that our followers feel motivated, inspired, and connected to the brand, measured via polls, DMs, or story replies

    TARGET AUDIENCE

  • Female field hockey players aged 16-26
  • Passionate about sport and self-improvement
  • Strong team values but also crave self-expression.
  • Seek products that reflect identity and boost performance.
  • Motivated by community, empowerment, and visual storytelling.

    • STRATEGY

    The campaign is exclusively on Instagram, based on direct feedback from our target audience during interviews. The participants confirmed that Instagram is their primary platform for content, both personal and team-based which makes it the most effective channel to reach and engage with them.We also explored various reliable resources to better understand our target group and their preferences. Based on both online and personal research, we concluded that Instagram is the ideal platform to promote our brand.

    1.Visual feel:

    Bold colours, dynamic action shots, and clean product photography ensure that every post reinforces our identity as a brand that combines power and style. The layout creates a balance between emotional storytelling (team moment, action) and product value (design, customization)

    2.Caption:

    Our captions are intentionally fun, motivational, and energetic. They speak directly to our audience with a confident tone that encourages them to go beyond their limits, embrace their uniqueness, and feel proud.

    • Learning Points

    Key learnings:

    • Make a content calendar: We learned that planning content in advance is essential for consistency, reduce stress, and smooth collaboration. This was actually our first time creating and working with a content calendar, and it proved to be one of the most valuable tools during the campaign. It helped us stay organized, meet our posting goals, and maintain a consistent brand presence on Instagram. By clearly mapping our weekly posts ahead of time and preparing them, we avoided last minute confusion and created a better flow of content.
    • We expected it to be easier: At the beginning of the project, we underestimated the time and effort required to produce quality content, maintaining engagement, and monitor analytics. This proved to be more time consuming than expected and was a valuable reality check that improved our planning for the future.
    • Communication within the team: Strong communication within our team is very important. During the whole project, we communicated tasks, ideas, and updates. Due to that, our workflow was smooth and successful. We shared the workload between all of us in order to present the best content and engagement with our target audience. One key learning from this was that clear, consistent internal communication directly impact the overall success of the campaign. It helped us stay organized, avoid misunderstandings, and deliver a more unified and polished result.
    • Tools we used: Helpful tools included Canva for fast and cohesive graphic design, Instagram Insights for tracking performance, and shared documents for the content calendar. All of those tools ensured a smooth workflow, consistent branding, and clear team communication throughout the campaign.

    What worked well:

    • The content Calendar: Because of our content calendar, we managed to publish content consistently 3 times a week as planned. This consistency helped with visibility and clear and organized planning and performance.
    • Communicating brand indentity: through consistent visuals and brand voice, we positioned our brand as a bold, feminine, team centred and sporty brand. The post and the captions that we created reflect our values and created a strong brand identity.

    Lean Canvas

    Problem

    • Right now there is no solution for the problem of the mouthguard falling out. The only option there is, is to get a personalized expensive one at the dentist.
    • Users have the option of buying a flavored mouthguard, however this is a very unknown solution. The flavors that are on the market are very chemical/ artificial. Besides, after wearing this the taste fades away.
    • Now users can use hot water to mold their plastic mouthguard to fit their teeth. This just doesn't work considering it would never get a full proper fit like it would with a mold at the dentist. Besides, making your mouthguard at the dentist is a really expensive alternative around 150 euro‚Äôs.
    • Most of the mouthguard on the market are a plain color or a basic design.

      • Solution

    • With the vacuum system, the mouthguard sucks in while pressing on the bottom on the side. When you want to release the vacuum you have to press again. This way the mouthguard stay in its place and does not fall out during the match.
    • Using a paste with a flavor that you put in your mouthguard with a small amount before the match. This way you can have a different taste every match. The paste is based on natural products and therefore not harmful to the teeth, while ensuring a subtle and not a chemical taste.
    • The costumer will get a DIY kit at home, to make their own mold from their teeth. This mold will be send back to our company where we can develop a complete fitting mouthguard adjusted to their own teeth.
    • Offer a broad choice of different patterns and colors, besides there is also an option to create your own design.

    Validation of Assumptions

    Key pivots based unique value proposition

    The interviews helped improve the original value proposition by directly addressing the real frustrations and needs of female field hockey players. Players like Linde, Fenne, and Mara highlighted how uncomfortable and unstable traditional mouthguards are, which led to the introduction of a vacuum-fit system for better comfort and security. Emma, Isabel, and Marieke raised concerns about bad taste and hygiene, prompting the addition of a flavored paste for freshness.

    Beyond functionality, players such as Isabel, Christa, and Emma emphasized the emotional side of the game. These girls wanted to feel confident and supported as women in sport. This inspired a shift in tone to focus on empowerment, using words like ‚Äúfearless,‚Äù ‚Äúprotected,‚Äù and ‚Äúunrestricted.‚Äù Lastly, feedback from Mara, Robin, and Fenne showed a desire for more expressive, fun gear, especially among younger athletes, which led to bolder branding, personalization options, and a youth-oriented positioning.

    With the feedback we got through the interviews we refined our business plan by focusing on a clear target audience which went from 11-25 to 16-26. We made a change in the problems we addressed and created a unique and relevant solution. This way we could develop a strategy that aligns with how our target audience truly thinks and behaves.
